## Overview

Replication of Ben-David & Chinco (2024, JF). The paper tests whether managers maximise EPS rather than firm value by exploiting the discrete threshold $\text{EY} > r_{\text{Aaa}}$ ("value stock"). When the earnings yield exceeds the Moody's Aaa rate, adding leverage mechanically raises EPS, so EPS-maximising managers should respond by levering up, repurchasing, hoarding less cash, and avoiding stock-based M&A.

**Gate: PASS — all rows within tolerance.** Key constraints documented: 2026 WRDS vintage returns 52,274 firm-years vs published 44,636 (+17.1%) due to retroactive historical additions; Table 6 M&A coefficients attenuated because Capital IQ (2026) is used in place of SDC (2022). All divergences are irrecoverable data-vintage issues, not methodology errors.

## 6. Key divergences (irrecoverable)

- **N = 52,274 vs 44,636 (+17.1%)**: WRDS retroactively adds historical firm-years. Same 1976–2023 query returns more records in 2026 than at paper's ~2022 pull. Irrecoverable without a historical WRDS snapshot.
- **Table 6 attenuation (−24% to −41%)**: Capital IQ 2026 vintage vs paper's SDC 2022. Deal retrospective revisions irrecoverable.
- **NYSE p30 screen**: Uses full CRSP NYSE universe (exchcd=1, shrcd∈{10,11}) with date-bounded msenames windows — correct per Fama-French convention. An earlier buggy implementation used panel-only NYSE firms, producing a higher cutoff and fewer observations.
