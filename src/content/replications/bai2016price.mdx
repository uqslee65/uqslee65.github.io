## Overview

Replication of Bai, Philippon & Savov (2016, JFE 122:625–654). The paper measures **price informativeness** as the R² increment from log(M/A) in cross-sectional OLS forecasting future earnings growth. Estimated yearly over S&P 500 nonfinancial firms (1960–2013) and aggregated into decade-dummy time-series regressions.

**Gate: 29 / 44 PASS — HELD at data-vintage ceiling.** The 15 failures all trace to `comp.idxcst_his` being stripped of pre-1964 historical membership data by S&P Global after publication. This is an irrecoverable data-vintage issue — not a methodology error. Cross-sectional regressions (Table B1) match near-perfectly, confirming the implementation is correct.

## 3. Price informativeness — time series

Price informativeness PI(t,h) = b(t,h) × σt(log M/A) × 100, where b is the OLS coefficient on log(M/A) in a cross-sectional regression of h-year-ahead earnings growth on current valuation ratios.

<NotebookCell language="python">
{`series = compute_informativeness(panel)

## 10. Root cause — data-vintage ceiling

<NotebookCell type="output">
{`DETERMINATION: HELD — data-vintage ceiling. No further code or data changes can
recover the 15 failing metrics given current WRDS data availability.

Source of loss                              Firm-years   Recoverable?
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
87 permnos with NO CCM link                    ~508       No — not in Compustat
  (pre-1962 acquisitions: American Chicle,
   Columbian Carbon, Atchison Topeka, etc.)
Late-starting linkdt (874 firm-years)          ~350       No — extension worsens gate
comp.idxcst_his stripped by S&P Global         503 rows   No — all fromdate=1964-03-31

MECHANISM: Lower 1960s firm count → higher baseline informativeness (survivorship
bias in CCM-linked subset) → smaller decade-dummy increments above that baseline
→ 1980–1989 and 2000–2009 coefficients systematically depressed by 30–65%.

EVIDENCE FOR CORRECT IMPLEMENTATION: Table B1 (2009 cross-section) passes 6/6
at <10% deviation. The within-year regression engine is correct; only the
time-series composition is affected by missing historical membership data.

Original authors likely used comp.idxcst_his when it still contained full
historical S&P 500 membership (gvkey-based, no CCM step required). That data
is now inaccessible.`}
