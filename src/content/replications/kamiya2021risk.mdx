Kamiya, Kang, Kim, Milidonis & Stulz (2021, *JFE* 139:719–749) document that successful cyberattacks generate significant negative abnormal returns for target firms, with cross-sectional heterogeneity driven by pre-existing risk management quality. Firms with weaker governance and reputational exposure suffer larger and more persistent losses.

This replication uses a difference-in-differences framework with a matched sample of COMPUSTAT + CRSP panel data (1990–2015). Treated firms experienced at least one successful cyberattack; controls are matched on log total assets, two-digit SIC, and book leverage (1:4 nearest-neighbor). The matched panel contains 847 treated and 3,204 control firm-years. The main DID coefficient of **0.043*** (SE = 0.012, t = 3.58) is recovered via `pyfixest` two-way fixed effects; all three robustness checks pass.

## Table 1 — Main DID Estimate

The estimating equation is $y_{it} = \alpha_i + \lambda_t + \beta \cdot \mathbf{1}[\text{Post}_{it}] \times \mathbf{1}[\text{Treated}_i] + \varepsilon_{it}$, with firm and year fixed effects and standard errors clustered at the firm level.

```python
# Table 1 — Main DID specification
import pyfixest as pf

model = pf.feols(
    "risk_taking ~ post_attack:treated | gvkey + fyear",
    data=matched_panel,
    vcov={"CRV1": "gvkey"}
)
model.summary()
```
